A position vector is a [vector]($v/vector) from the origin to a [point]($p/point)
which represents the position at that point. For a point
$$P = (p_1, \ldots, p_n)$$, its position vector is given by

$$
  \langle p_1, \ldots, p_n \rangle - \vect{0} = \langle p_1, \ldots, p_n \rangle
$$
